MATERIAL COMPLIANCE ISSUES
BP 5030: Student Wellness
Last Reviewed: 06/26/2019
================================================================================

Missing Specific Nutritional Standards for Foods and Beverages (Confidence: 90%)
Missing from: BOTH
While the policy references that nutrition guidelines shall be consistent with federal regulations, it does not specify the actual nutritional standards required by California Education Code 49431.2 for competitive foods sold on campus.

Legal Basis:
- Education Code Section 49431.2: Requires specific nutritional standards for all food sold to students during school hours, including limits on calories, sodium, fat, and sugar content.

Required Language:
Specific nutritional standards must be included detailing calorie limits, sodium content, fat percentages, and sugar limits for all competitive foods and beverages sold on campus during school hours, consistent with state and federal Smart Snacks standards.

Recommended Placement: AR

----------------------------------------

Missing Physical Education Minutes Requirements (Confidence: 85%)
Missing from: BOTH
The policy mentions physical activity opportunities but does not specify the minimum instructional minutes for physical education required by California Education Code 51210 and 51222.

Legal Basis:
- Education Code Sections 51210, 51222: Elementary schools must provide 200 minutes of physical education every 10 school days, and middle schools must provide 400 minutes every 10 school days.

Required Language:
The district shall provide physical education for a minimum of 200 minutes every 10 school days in elementary grades and 400 minutes every 10 school days in middle school grades, as required by Education Code sections 51210 and 51222.

Recommended Placement: AR
