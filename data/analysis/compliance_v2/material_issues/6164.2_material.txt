MATERIAL COMPLIANCE ISSUES
BP 6164.2: Guidance/Counseling Services
Last Reviewed: 03/08/2023
================================================================================

Missing Specific Procedures for Confidentiality and Record-Keeping (Confidence: 90%)
Missing from: BOTH
While BP mentions confidentiality requirements under Ed Code 49602, neither document provides specific procedures for handling, storing, and disclosing confidential counseling information, which is required for compliance.

Legal Basis:
- Education Code 49602: Information of a personal nature disclosed by a student 12 years or older is confidential and shall not become part of the student record without written consent, with specific exceptions for disclosure

Required Language:
Specific procedures for: (1) Securing and storing confidential counseling information separately from student records; (2) Obtaining written consent for disclosure; (3) Documenting exceptions to confidentiality; (4) Training staff on confidentiality requirements; (5) Procedures for when disclosure is required by law.

Recommended Placement: AR

----------------------------------------

Missing Mandatory Reporting Procedures (Confidence: 85%)
Missing from: BOTH
Neither document addresses mandatory reporting requirements for counselors when they become aware of child abuse, neglect, or threats of harm, which are legally required procedures.

Legal Basis:
- Penal Code 11164-11174.3 (Child Abuse and Neglect Reporting Act): School counselors are mandated reporters and must report suspected child abuse or neglect within specified timeframes

Required Language:
Procedures for mandatory reporting including: (1) Recognition of signs of abuse/neglect; (2) Immediate reporting requirements and timelines; (3) Documentation procedures; (4) Follow-up requirements; (5) Training for counseling staff on mandatory reporting obligations.

Recommended Placement: AR

----------------------------------------

Missing Specific Consent Procedures for Mental Health Services (Confidence: 88%)
Missing from: BOTH
While BP mentions written parent consent is required for mental health services, neither document provides specific procedures for obtaining, documenting, and maintaining such consent, or exceptions when students can self-consent.

Legal Basis:
- Family Code 6920-6929: Specifies when minors can consent to mental health treatment without parental consent

Required Language:
Detailed procedures for: (1) Obtaining written parental consent for mental health services; (2) Determining when students age 12+ can self-consent; (3) Documentation and record-keeping of consent; (4) Procedures when consent is refused; (5) Emergency mental health intervention procedures.

Recommended Placement: AR
