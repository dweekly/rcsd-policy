MATERIAL COMPLIANCE ISSUES
BP 5131.9: Academic Honesty
Last Reviewed: 01/13/2010
================================================================================

Missing Due Process Protections (Confidence: 90%)
Missing from: BOTH
Neither the policy nor regulation contains specific due process protections required when disciplining students for academic dishonesty, including notice requirements, hearing procedures, and appeal rights.

Legal Basis:
- Education Code Section 48911: The pupil and the pupil's parent or guardian shall be notified of the pupil's right to a hearing before the principal or the principal's designee at which the pupil and the pupil's parent or guardian may present the pupil's version of the incident.

Required Language:
Students accused of academic dishonesty shall receive written notice of the allegations and have the right to a hearing before disciplinary action is taken. The notice shall include the specific charges, evidence, potential consequences, and the student's right to present their version of events. Parents/guardians must be notified and may participate in the hearing process.

Recommended Placement: AR

----------------------------------------

Missing Specific Definitions of Academic Dishonesty (Confidence: 85%)
Missing from: BOTH
The policy mentions cheating, lying, and plagiarism but lacks comprehensive definitions that would help students, staff, and parents understand what constitutes academic dishonesty in various contexts.

Legal Basis:
- Education Code Section 35291: Rules for student discipline must be clearly defined to ensure fair and consistent application.

Required Language:
Academic dishonesty includes but is not limited to: (1) Cheating - using unauthorized materials, information, or assistance on academic work; (2) Plagiarism - presenting another's work, ideas, or words as one's own without proper attribution; (3) Fabrication - falsifying or inventing information, data, or citations; (4) Unauthorized collaboration - working with others when individual work is required; (5) Multiple submission - submitting the same work for multiple assignments without permission.

Recommended Placement: AR
