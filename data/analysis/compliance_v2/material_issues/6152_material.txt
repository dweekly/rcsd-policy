MATERIAL COMPLIANCE ISSUES
BP 6152: Class Assignment
Last Reviewed: 06/23/2010
================================================================================

Missing Nondiscrimination Statement in Class Assignment (Confidence: 90%)
Missing from: BOTH
Class assignment policies must include explicit nondiscrimination language to ensure compliance with state and federal civil rights requirements. The current policy lacks any reference to prohibited discrimination in class assignments.

Legal Basis:
- Education Code Section 220: No person shall be subjected to discrimination on the basis of disability, gender, gender identity, gender expression, nationality, race or ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation, or any other characteristic that is contained in the definition of hate crimes set forth in Section 422.55 of the Penal Code, including immigration status, in any program or activity conducted by an educational institution that receives, or benefits from, state financial assistance, or enrolls pupils who receive state student financial aid.

Required Language:
Class assignments shall be made without regard to the student's actual or perceived disability, gender, gender identity, gender expression, nationality, race or ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation, immigration status, or association with a person or group with one or more of these actual or perceived characteristics.

Recommended Placement: BP

----------------------------------------

Missing Reference to Special Education Requirements (Confidence: 85%)
Missing from: BOTH
Class assignment policies must address requirements for students with disabilities, including IEP and 504 plan considerations, least restrictive environment principles, and appropriate accommodations in class placement decisions.

Legal Basis:
- Education Code Section 56040.1: It is the intent of the Legislature that special education programs be designed to meet the unique educational needs of individuals with exceptional needs and that such programs be of sufficient size, scope, and quality to meet the educational needs of such individuals.

Required Language:
Class assignments for students with disabilities shall be made in accordance with the student's individualized education program (IEP) or Section 504 plan, ensuring placement in the least restrictive environment appropriate to meet the student's needs.

Recommended Placement: BP
