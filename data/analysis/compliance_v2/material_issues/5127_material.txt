MATERIAL COMPLIANCE ISSUES
BP 5127: Graduation Ceremonies And Activities
Last Reviewed: 01/13/2010
================================================================================

Incomplete Due Process Procedures for Denial of Participation (Confidence: 90%)
Missing from: BOTH
While the policy mentions that students must be given notice and opportunity to respond before being denied participation in graduation ceremonies, it lacks specific procedural requirements for due process, including timelines, hearing procedures, and appeal rights that meet constitutional standards.

Legal Basis:
- Education Code Section 48900 et seq.: Establishes due process requirements for disciplinary actions affecting student privileges

Required Language:
Specific procedures must include: written notice at least 5 days prior to ceremony; specific grounds for denial; opportunity for informal hearing; right to representation; timeline for appeals; and designation of appeal authority.

Recommended Placement: AR
