MATERIAL COMPLIANCE ISSUES
BP 3513.4: Drug And Alcohol Free Schools
Last Reviewed: 08/25/2021
================================================================================

Missing Specific Disciplinary Standards and Sanctions (Confidence: 90%)
Missing from: BOTH
Federal law requires clear specification of disciplinary sanctions for violations. The policy only states students "may be subject to discipline" without detailing specific consequences or progressive discipline standards.

Legal Basis:
- 20 USC 7114(d)(1)(A): Standards of conduct that clearly prohibit, at a minimum, the unlawful possession, use, or distribution of illicit drugs and alcohol by students and employees on school premises or as part of any school activities.

Required Language:
Clear statement of disciplinary sanctions that will be imposed on students and employees who violate the standards of conduct, including but not limited to: suspension, expulsion, referral to law enforcement, and mandatory participation in drug/alcohol abuse assistance or rehabilitation programs.

Recommended Placement: AR

----------------------------------------

Missing Health Risks and Legal Consequences Information (Confidence: 85%)
Missing from: BOTH
Federal law requires the policy to include information about health risks associated with drug and alcohol use and legal sanctions under local, state, and federal law.

Legal Basis:
- 20 USC 7114(d)(1)(B)(i)(ii): A description of the health risks associated with the use of illicit drugs and the abuse of alcohol; and a description of any drug or alcohol counseling, treatment, or rehabilitation or re-entry programs that are available.

Required Language:
Description of health risks associated with illicit drug use and alcohol abuse, applicable legal sanctions under local, state and federal law, and available drug/alcohol counseling, treatment, rehabilitation and re-entry programs.

Recommended Placement: AR
