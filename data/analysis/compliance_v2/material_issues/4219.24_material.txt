MATERIAL COMPLIANCE ISSUES
BP 4219.24: Maintaining Appropriate Adult-Student Interactions
Last Reviewed: 11/20/2019
================================================================================

Missing Specific Complaint Investigation Procedures (Confidence: 90%)
Missing from: BOTH
Education Code 44050 specifically requires procedures for receiving and investigating complaints. While the BP mentions reporting to the Superintendent, it lacks detailed procedural requirements for how complaints will be processed, investigated, and resolved.

Legal Basis:
- Education Code Section 44050(b): The policy shall include procedures for receiving and investigating complaints alleging violations of the policy.

Required Language:
Specific procedures must be established for: (1) How complaints are received and documented; (2) Who conducts investigations; (3) Investigation timelines; (4) Rights of complainants and accused parties; (5) Evidence gathering procedures; (6) Decision-making process; (7) Appeal procedures; (8) Documentation requirements.

Recommended Placement: AR

----------------------------------------

Missing Training Requirements (Confidence: 85%)
Missing from: BOTH
While the policy references providing the employee code of conduct to parents, there are no specific training requirements for employees, contractors, and volunteers on appropriate adult-student interactions as required for effective implementation.

Legal Basis:
- Education Code Section 44050: The governing board shall establish a policy on interactions between district employees and pupils that promotes professional relationships and prohibits conduct that could reasonably lead to inappropriate relationships.

Required Language:
Training requirements must specify: (1) Mandatory training for all employees, contractors, and volunteers; (2) Training content covering appropriate boundaries and prohibited conduct; (3) Frequency of training (initial and refresher); (4) Documentation of training completion; (5) Specialized training for supervisors on recognizing and addressing violations.

Recommended Placement: AR
