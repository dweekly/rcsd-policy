MATERIAL COMPLIANCE ISSUES
BP 5147: Dropout Prevention
Last Reviewed: 01/13/2010
================================================================================

Missing Student Success Team (SST) Process Requirements (Confidence: 90%)
Missing from: BOTH
California requires districts to establish Student Success Team processes for at-risk students as part of dropout prevention efforts. Neither the policy nor regulation addresses SST procedures, referral processes, or intervention planning requirements.

Legal Basis:
- Education Code Section 48321: Districts shall establish procedures for identifying students at risk of retention and provide appropriate intervention through student success teams

Required Language:
Procedures for Student Success Team referrals, composition, meeting requirements, intervention planning, and follow-up monitoring for students identified as at-risk of dropping out.

Recommended Placement: AR

----------------------------------------

Missing Parent/Guardian Notification Requirements (Confidence: 85%)
Missing from: BOTH
State law requires specific parent notification procedures when students are identified as at-risk of dropping out. Neither document addresses mandatory parent communication, notification timelines, or involvement in intervention planning.

Legal Basis:
- Education Code Section 48260.5: Parents/guardians must be notified when students are identified as at-risk and involved in intervention planning

Required Language:
Requirements for timely parent/guardian notification when students are identified as at-risk of dropping out, including notification methods, timelines, and procedures for involving parents in intervention planning and monitoring.

Recommended Placement: AR

----------------------------------------

Missing Data Collection and Reporting Requirements (Confidence: 80%)
Missing from: BOTH
Districts must collect and report specific data on dropout prevention efforts and outcomes. Neither document addresses data collection procedures, reporting requirements, or program evaluation metrics required by state law.

Legal Basis:
- Education Code Section 52052: Districts must collect and report data on student outcomes including dropout rates and intervention effectiveness as part of LCAP requirements

Required Language:
Procedures for collecting, analyzing, and reporting data on at-risk student identification, intervention implementation, and program effectiveness, including annual reporting requirements and program evaluation metrics.

Recommended Placement: AR

----------------------------------------
